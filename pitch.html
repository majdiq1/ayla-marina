Today

  • No digital map. A first-time visitor has to walk the property to discover what's there.
  • Tenants are invisible unless searched by exact name on Google Maps.
  • Foot traffic is uneven. Anchor businesses near entrances win; the rest get missed.

After this platform

  • Every tenant is one tap away from Directions, Call, WhatsApp, Instagram, Share.
  • Filters surface what visitors are actually looking for (cafe, pharmacy, kids' activity).
  • Real analytics: what's searched, what's clicked, peak hours, conversion to action.
  • One admin can update 55 listings in minutes — without a developer.

The visitor experience

A native app, without the app store.

Built as a Progressive Web App — installs to the home screen with one tap, works offline after first visit, no download required, no app store gatekeepers.

Search any place

Type "coffee" or "pharmacy" — the map flies to the result and opens its details.

Filter by category

Nine colour-coded categories. One tap shows only cafés, only shops, only experiences.

Directions in one tap

Opens Google Maps or Apple Maps with walking route already plotted.

Direct contact

Call, WhatsApp, Instagram, or share the venue with friends — all from the place card.

Mobile-native feel

iOS-style bottom tab bar, drag-to-dismiss sheets, haptics, springy transitions. Designed for the phone first.

Bilingual

English and Arabic, with full RTL layout. Switches with one tap or auto-detects from device locale.

Deep-linkable

Every place has its own URL — tenants share their own pin on Instagram or WhatsApp, driving footfall directly to their door.

Installable PWA

Visitors save "Ayla Marina" to their home screen. Splash screen, no browser chrome, feels native.

Module 2 · Analytics

For the first time, Ayla sees what visitors do.

Every tap, every search, every direction request is recorded. Ayla and tenants finally have real data on what's working and what's missing.

Attention heatmap · Marina level

What Ayla learns

  • Which businesses get attention and which need help with visibility (data for tenant performance reviews).
  • What visitors search for — including queries with zero results. "sushi" with 121 searches = an unmet demand and a leasing opportunity.
  • Peak hours per day, per category. Cafés peak at 10 AM; restaurants at 8 PM. Plan events, security, staffing accordingly.
  • Conversion funnel: 82 % of map views lead to a place tap, 48 % open directions, 21 % call or WhatsApp. Tenants get accountability metrics.
  • Source attribution: QR signage, Instagram, Google, saved app. Know which channels work for Ayla's marketing budget.

What tenants get

A monthly performance report PDF for each business: their pin views, contact actions, search terms that found them, peers in their category. Tenants pay for retail space at Ayla — they now see what they're paying for. This protects renewal rates.
